The Holy Spirit - Comforter

As Believers, we have to know about the important ministry of the Holy Spirit in our lives. He is our Divine Partner and in this teaching we will look at a few aspects of His ministry in us.

Jesus said in John 14, “And I will pray the Father, and He will give you another Helper (Comforter, Counsellor, Intercessor, Advocate, Strengthener, and Standby) that He may abide with you forever—”
(John 14:16)
This is an amazing statement by Jesus about the ministry of the Holy Spirit.

First, Jesus established the importance of the Holy Spirit by naming Him “another” Helper. Jesus was the primary Helper (Comforter) of the disciples up to that point. He was their “all in all”, but He said that even though He was going to His Father, He would not leave them alone. In some sense Jesus was going to ask the Father to send a “replacement” Helper to be with them forever. Think about it, who can really be a fitting replacement for Jesus? There is only One! The Holy Spirit! Jesus was saying that the Holy Spirit who would come after Him would be worthy to step into their lives to represent Him. The Holy Spirit has the same credentials, experience, attitude, love and power as Jesus! He is not beneath Jesus in Divine stature. Everything Jesus can do, the Holy Spirit can do too! That was what Jesus was saying!

Holy Spirit Interceder

Helper or Comforter in this context means “summoned or called to one’s side or aid” and was used to describe two powerful functions:

For one who pleads your case before a judge. The Holy Spirit is your Divine Attorney. He is your legal counsel in the spiritual realm. The Bible describes the enemy of our souls as “the accuser of the brethren”. He would come before the Lord to bring accusations against the elect of God. What an amazing thought that in such cases we are not destitute and without representation. The Holy Spirit pleads our case on our behalf! In addition to that, Jesus, Who is at the right hand of the Father already, joins the Holy Spirit and also pleads on our behalf because of the price He paid on the Cross for our sins.

Think of it this way: The Holy Spirit is on retainer to represent you before God.

It is also used for someone who pleads another’s cause. The Holy Spirit is a Divine Intercessor. We already know that Jesus intercedes on behalf of the saints in heaven, but now Jesus also describes the Holy Spirit as an Intercessor. We read in Romans 8: “Likewise the Spirit also helps in our weaknesses. For we do not know what we should pray for as we ought, but the Spirit Himself makes intercession for us with groanings which cannot be uttered.” The Holy Spirit “comes to our aid” (Romans 8:26) which means “to lay hold along with, to strive to obtain with others, to take hold with another”.

The Holy Spirit’s ministry

The Holy Spirit’s ministry in your life is to help you wrap your hands around what the Lord wants you to have. The Holy Spirit will help you take hold of the blessings and power of God! He helps you with things you are unable to carry or take hold of yourself.
Next He “helps in our weaknesses”. This phrase covers both physical and emotional sickness and weakness and literally means “lack of strength or understanding”. Remember that the Holy Spirit resides in you. That means you have a Divine Diagnostician and Healer in you. When you are sick, you pray for healing. You might mention a list of symptoms or names of sicknesses in your prayer, but the Holy Spirit has access to your body up to the atomic level! He can intercede for you on a level you would never be able to. He can say: “Cell number 2,537,567 in the right side of his chest needs healing because. . .”
The Holy Spirit’s intercession brings a divine perspective to your life. When you have a lack of strength to understand, He comes to your aid. Or when you lack strength to do great things, He steps in and partners with you. When you are too weak to restrain from corrupt desires, the Holy Spirit provides aid to see you through to victory.

Holy Spirit Intercession

This verse mentions a specific type of intercession. The Holy Spirit pleads on our behalf with unutterable groaning and yearnings. Within the context of Romans 8 the meaning becomes obvious.
Paul writes in Romans 8 that “For we know that the whole creation groans and labours with birth pangs together until now.” (Romans 8:22). He is speaking of contractions in labour and how the whole earth was in labour for the life which is to come.
The Holy Spirit intercedes through travail on our behalf. He is in a “birthing process” when He prays for us. How can this be? Well, He labours on our behalf for new life. He is ready to birth the answers to your questions, the healing for your sickness, the provision for your lack, the power for your lack of strength, etc. Isn’t this wonderful! Not only does the Holy Spirit identify and diagnose what you need, but He also meets your every need by “birthing it” into your spirit. He is the initiator of new life in you. He says things you cannot find word for.

What an awesome God we serve!
