The Human Body

I will praise You, for I am fearfully and wonderfully made; Marvellous are Your works, and that my soul knows very well. (Psa 139:14)

The brain itself cannot feel pain. While the brain might be the pain centre when you cut your finger or burn yourself, the brain itself does not have pain receptors.

Your left lung is smaller than your right lung to make room for your heart.

The average human produces 1.1 litres of saliva a day or 454000 litres in a lifetime.

In one hour, your heart works hard enough to produce the equivalent energy to raise almost 1 ton of mass 1 m off the ground.

Scientists say the higher your I.Q. the more you dream.

The average human's heart will beat 3000 million times in their lifetime.

There are 72 km of nerves in the skin of a human being.

A full bladder is roughly the size of a soft ball (305 mm in circumference).

Your brain operates on the same amount of power that would light a 10-watt light bulb.

There are 5 million hair follicles on an average adult.

The acid in your stomach is strong enough to dissolve razorblades.

The human brain cell can hold 5 times as much information as the Encyclopaedia Britannica.

Approximately 75% of human faeces is made of water.

Science agrees that the eye is far too complex to have evolved.

Your eyes are always the same size from birth but your nose and ears never stop growing.

The average human dream lasts 2-3 seconds.

Your brain is more active during the night than the day.

Human thigh bones are stronger than concrete.

The tooth is the only part of the human body that can't repair itself.

An average human loses about 200 head hairs per day.

The white part of your fingernail is called the Lunula.

Fingernails grow nearly 4 times faster than toenails.

Your thumb is approximately the same size as your nose.

A woman's heart beats faster than a man's.

It only takes 3.1 kg of pressure to rip off your ears.

There are nine muscles in your ear.

Your nose can remember 50,000 different scents.

If the average male never shaved, his beard would be 3.9 m long when he died.

Nerve impulses to and from the brain travel as fast as 273 km per hour.

There are about one trillion bacteria on each of your feet.

There are no two tongue prints that are alike.

Injured fingernails grow faster than uninjured ones.

Women blink twice as much as men.

Most people blink about 25 times a minute.

When you are looking at someone you love, your pupils dilate, they do the same when you are looking at someone you hate.

Your body gives off enough heat in 30 minutes to bring 2.0 litres of water to a boil.

Blondes have more hair than dark-haired people.

The largest internal organ is the small intestine. The small intestine is four times as long as the average adult is tall.

Facial hair grows faster than any other hair on the body.

Bone is stronger than the steel (for every 25mm) used in skyscrapers.

About one third of the human race has 20-20 vision.

The enamel in your teeth is the hardest substance in your body.

Your teeth start growing 6 months before you are born.

You're ears secrete more earwax when you are afraid than when you aren't.

Your body uses 300 muscles to balance itself when you are standing still.

The average woman is 127 mm shorter than the average man.

The ashes of a cremated person average about 4.0 kg. A big part of what gives the human body weight is the water trapped in our cells.

After reading these few facts about the human body, listed above, we can only agree with Psa 139:14 that our Lord is marvellous!
