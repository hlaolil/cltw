On-line Writing Labs

Sites who offer interactive language learning

See also the sites listed under Self-test.

  • Grammar Clinic Frame - An on-line grammar clinic from England. Administered by the Lydbury Centre.
  • National Writing Centers Association (NWCA)
    Writing Center Handouts     A directory of online writing center handouts. Lists sites that provide indexes to their handouts. And, provides an index by subject for those sites with no index of their own. Covers everything from organizing and developing ideas to grammar and punctuation to documenting sources and plagiarism to using non-sexist language.
  • National Writing Centers Association (NWCA)
    Writing Centers Online     Strives to be a comprehensive list of Writing Center sites.
  • OWLs on the Web - (Online Writing Labs) Links primarily to the “handouts” pages of the more comprehensive OWLs (Online Writing Labs) on the Web. A selection of these handouts have been organized by topic and directly linked in the Links for Writing section of this manual. Before going to these large sites, you might try looking through the OWL Handouts by Topic.
  • OWL Handouts (Purdue University) Purdue has one of the largest OWLs on the Web, covering everything from writing in the job search to punctuation, parts of speech, and spelling to English as a Second Language. This links you to a list of over 130 online “handouts” on research and writing, categorized by subject. For additional writing resources on the Web, go to Purdue's
  • RSCC Online Writing Lab (Roane State Community College) One of the better OWLs (Online Writing Labs) on the Web. A large collection of articles and links on writing. Covers composition, documentation, research, editing, grammar, punctuation, and more.
  • Salt Lake Community College Online Writing Center
  • National Writing Centers Association
  • EnglishPage.com Language Dynamics Free online English lessons and exercises for intermediate and advanced learners of English as a foreign language.
  • English Zone Grammar Kaye Mastin Mallory of Language Systems -- International College of English, California Many quizzes and exercises covering articles, compounds, nouns, confusing words, pronouns, adjectives, conditionals, qualifiers, questions, time and money, agreeing, clauses and prepositions.
  • Exploring English Greg Dixon, Knowledge Explorer Centre, published by Shared Visions Unlimited, North Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada. "Explore the wonders and quirks of the English Language including the parts of speech, sentences, style guidelines, usage, punctuation, spelling, and more."
  • Grammar Handbook Writers' Workshop, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. Explains and illustrates the basic grammatical rules for parts of speech, phrases, clauses, sentences and sentence elements, and common problems of usage.
  • HyperGrammar Writing Centre, University of Ottawa, Canada Includes sections on the parts of speech and sentences; using pronouns, verbs and modifiers; building phrases, clauses and sentences; and writing paragraphs.
  • Internet Grammar of English University College London. Online course on word classes, phrases, clauses, sentences, form and function.
  • Self-Help Handouts Writing Center, Tidewater Community College, South Hampton Roads, Virginia Guidelines on using the parts of speech and punctuation marks. Also includes guidelines on organizing paragraphs, overcoming writer's block and the writing process.
  • Superteach: Your English Language Grammar Tutor On-Line Lindsay Lyons, ABCheckers, an English language consultancy set up in 1995 by a group of New Zealand teachers. Free online English grammar lessons on verb forms, sentence fragments and "What is a sentence?"
  • Traditional Grammar: An Interactive Book Donald E. Hardy, associate professor, Department of English, Northern Illinois University An introduction to the basic syntactic structure of modern English and the most common prescriptive errors in formal writing and how to avoid them -- with thousands of exercises to aid learning.
  • The Writer's Complex Workshop Empire State College, New York Explanations and exercises on essay writing, punctuation, grammar and style
  • Writing Exercises for Engineers and Scientists editors from Virginia Tech, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign and University of Texas at Austin Interactive writing exercises to help engineers and scientists with grammar, punctuation, usage, style and knowledge of written English.
  • Purdue Online Writing Center
  • On-Line English Grammar - alphabetical listing of topics and explanations by Anthony Hughes, St. John's Wood School of English, London England. Contains English language practice pages and helps.
  • Rensellaer Writing Center
  • Online Writing Lab From self-instructional materials on basic skills to pedagogical resources for teachers of writing across the curriculum. The site grew out of the Purdue Writing Lab's use of e-mail to distribute writing-related handouts. The Web pages now contain some 150 handouts on skills ranging from spelling to punctuation and research citation form.
